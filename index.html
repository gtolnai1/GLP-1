How DXA Works

Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry

A visual guide for A-Level Biology

1. Dual Energy Beams

The machine sends two distinct X-ray beams through the body: one at a high energy level and one at a low energy level.

2. Tissue Absorption (Attenuation)

As the beams pass through the patient, different tissues absorb (attenuate) the X-rays differently. Bone absorbs a lot of X-rays, while fat and muscle let more pass through.

3. Detection & Subtraction

The detector above measures how much of each beam makes it through. By using a mathematical algorithm to compare the high-energy vs. low-energy absorption, the computer "subtracts" the soft tissue to accurately calculate bone density, and differentiates fat from lean muscle mass.

Fat Mass

DXA accurately maps stored lipid tissue. Because fat absorbs X-rays differently than muscle, the dual beams create a highly precise map of body fat percentage and distribution.

Lean Mass

The scan isolates functional muscle tissue and water. This is why DXA is the "gold standard" in studies measuring whether GLP-1 medications cause muscle loss alongside fat loss.

Bone Mineral Density

Bones are dense with calcium and absorb the most X-ray radiation. DXA is most famous for diagnosing osteoporosis by calculating exactly how dense and strong the bones are.
